## Objective

Become the strongest independent English-language Dunhuang resource by combining practical trip planning, cited cultural knowledge, visual interpretation and responsible modern reflection. “Number one” is pursued by query cluster, not claimed as a guaranteed single universal ranking.

## Current baseline

- 117 static pages after round one; 99 content articles.
- Google already surfaces the homepage, Dunhuang hub, travel guide, murals, itinerary, spiritual-jewelry and IDP pages for relevant searches.
- The largest constraint is uneven depth: many legacy articles are below 300 words and several Dunhuang pages below 250 words.
- Strong technical foundation: static Astro output, canonical URLs, XML sitemap, robots file, article/breadcrumb/FAQ schema and editorial trust pages.

## Topic architecture

1. Travel decisions: transport, duration, accommodation, itineraries, weather, packing and booking workflow.
2. Places: Mogao, Mingsha, Crescent Lake, Yumen Pass, Yang Pass, Yardang, museum, Yulin Caves and Western Thousand Buddha Caves.
3. Cave and art database: important numbered caves, periods, mural subjects, sculpture, pigments and conservation.
4. Manuscripts and people: Cave 17, Diamond Sutra, languages, Wang Yuanlu, Aurel Stein, Paul Pelliot, Dunhuang Academy and IDP.
5. Silk Road context: geography, dynasties, religions, trade, languages and frontier systems.
6. Responsible symbolism: Feitian, lotus, Three Hares, Nine-Colored Deer and historically grounded modern interpretation.

## 90-day sequence

### Days 1–30: own the travel-planning cluster

- Publish or deepen 25–35 pages covering major attractions, trip lengths, transport legs and visitor questions.
- Upgrade every travel page to a concrete-answer standard: direct answer, decision criteria, current-verification note, internal links, FAQ and sources where factual claims require them.
- Add a dedicated visual travel hub and route-based internal navigation.
- Review Search Console weekly for queries with impressions and positions 8–30; improve those pages before creating adjacent URLs.

### Days 31–60: build the English Dunhuang knowledge base

- Create 40–60 carefully sourced pages around caves, murals, manuscripts, dynasties and named stories.
- Use primary sources from UNESCO, Dunhuang Academy, IDP and holding museums whenever possible.
- Add image captions, provenance notes and “what is documented vs interpretation” boxes.
- Merge or noindex legacy pages that remain thin, overlap strongly or do not reinforce Dunhuang topical authority.

### Days 61–90: authority and digital PR

- Publish linkable assets: cave chronology, manuscript map, glossary, responsible image guide and downloadable itinerary.
- Pitch relevant travel, museum, Buddhist-art and Silk Road publications with original resources rather than generic guest posts.
- Build author/editorial profiles with named expertise or external review where available.
- Refresh high-impression pages using actual query data; keep time-sensitive prices and opening hours out unless a reliable maintenance workflow exists.

## Quality gate for every new indexable URL

- One distinct search intent and a direct answer in the opening section.
- At least three pieces of page-specific information that cannot be swapped into a neighboring template.
- Useful structure, original synthesis and at least two contextual internal links.
- Primary-source support for historical or institutional claims when available.
- No invented spiritual, medical, ticket, schedule or access claims.
- Index only when the page is meaningfully better than a section on an existing page.

## Measurement

- Weekly: indexed pages, non-indexed reasons, impressions, clicks, CTR, average position and top new queries.
- Cluster level: number of queries in top 3, top 10 and top 20; non-brand clicks; pages receiving organic entrances.
- Quality: thin-page count, orphan-page count, source coverage and query cannibalization.
- First 30-day target: establish 10–20 travel and culture queries in the top 20 and lift clicks on pages already receiving impressions. Rankings depend on crawl, competition and authority, so URL count is not used as the primary success metric.
