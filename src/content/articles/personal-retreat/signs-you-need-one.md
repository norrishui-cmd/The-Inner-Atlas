## These are signs, not a diagnosis

None of the following mean something is wrong with you. They are common signals that your days have left little room for unscheduled thought—which a retreat is well suited to address.

## Common signs

- You answer messages instantly but can't say what you actually think about a decision.
- Your calendar is full, yet you can't remember the last time you were bored on purpose.
- A small inconvenience triggers a reaction that feels bigger than the moment.
- You keep postponing a conversation with yourself about a change you already sense is coming.
- Rest doesn't feel restful, because your mind stays in the same reactive mode even when your body stops.

## What a retreat does and doesn't fix

A retreat creates space to notice and think clearly. It is not medical or psychological treatment, and it does not resolve underlying mental health conditions. If distress is persistent, severe, or affects your safety or daily functioning, that calls for a qualified professional rather than a self-directed retreat.

## If this sounds familiar

Start smaller than a full retreat: try [one protected day](/personal-retreat/one-day/) before committing to a longer stretch of time.
