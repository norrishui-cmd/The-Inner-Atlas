## The retreat is the structure, not the venue

A retreat's value comes from a protected block of time with a clear intention—not from a paid facility. Once you separate those two things, cost stops being the main barrier.

## Near-zero-cost options

- **Your own home**, on a day when you can genuinely be undisturbed—arrange coverage for anyone who depends on you.
- **A public park, beach, or forest** for a day retreat with no entry fee.
- **A library or quiet religious or community space** that allows visitors to sit for extended periods.
- **A friend's empty apartment or cabin** for a night, in exchange for reciprocal use of your own space later.

## Where it's worth spending a little

If budget allows any spending at all, prioritize removing a distraction rather than adding comfort: a single night away from your usual environment often does more than an elaborate setup at home.

## Keep the structure simple

Bring a notebook, water, simple food prepared in advance, and a short, honest intention. The [step-by-step planning guide](/personal-retreat/how-to-plan/) works the same whether the setting cost nothing or a great deal.
