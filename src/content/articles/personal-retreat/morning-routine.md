## Why the first hour matters

The first hour sets the tone for the rest of a retreat day. Rushing straight into tasks—even reflective ones—can recreate the same pace you were trying to leave behind.

## A simple sequence

1. **Wake without an alarm if possible**, or set one later than your usual weekday time.
2. **Leave your phone off or in another room** for at least the first hour.
3. **Drink water and step outside**, even briefly, before anything else.
4. **Move gently**—a short walk or stretching, not exercise as a task to complete.
5. **Write one honest sentence** about how you actually feel this morning, not how you expect to feel.

## What to skip

Skip email, news, and planning the rest of the day in detail during this hour. Those can wait until the routine is finished.

## Repeating it

If your retreat runs more than one day, use a shorter version of this same sequence each morning. Consistency, not novelty, is what makes it useful—see our [step-by-step planning guide](/personal-retreat/how-to-plan/) for how this fits into the rest of the day.
