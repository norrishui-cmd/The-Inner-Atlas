## A retreat is a pace, not a promise

Dunhuang’s desert, oasis, and cave landscapes can create conditions for reflection. They do not guarantee spiritual transformation. Build a retreat around enough time, respectful access, hydration, and attention to the living communities and heritage sites you visit.

## A gentle two-day arc

On day one, arrive slowly and spend time with the Mogao Caves before writing what you actually saw. On day two, walk near Crescent Lake or another permitted landscape at a safe hour, then sit with the changing light. Leave room between locations; the gaps are part of the practice.

## Carry a small anchor

A notebook, a bead, or a symbol card can help you remember the question you brought. Keep it ordinary and transparent. The point is to return to observation when the journey ends, not to claim an object or landscape has cured anything.
