## Start with one object

An object earns a place in meditation when it helps you return. A small image, a manuscript-inspired journal, a bead strand, or a piece of cloth can all work. Choose one whose history is described honestly and whose care feels sustainable.

## A four-part arrangement

Place the object at eye level, keep a clear surface for your hands, add a notebook for observations, and leave enough empty space to breathe. The arrangement is a personal study corner, not a reconstructed shrine. Do not present commercial objects as relics.

## Use, then review

Sit for five minutes, notice one detail, and write one line. After a week, ask whether the object is helping you pay attention or merely adding visual noise. Keep the practice; change the object if needed.
