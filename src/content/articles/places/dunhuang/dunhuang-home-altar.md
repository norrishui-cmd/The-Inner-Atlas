## A corner for attention, not a replica shrine

A modern Dunhuang-inspired altar should not pretend to reproduce a sacred Buddhist space. Use it as a small reading and reflection corner: a place for a book, a journal, a single image with its caption, and one object that helps you remember a chosen practice.

## A respectful arrangement

Choose a stable surface with natural light. Add one documented image or reproduction, clearly labeled with its source. Place a notebook beside it. If you use incense, follow local safety guidance and avoid treating smoke as a cure. Leave empty space; the quiet is part of the arrangement.

## What to avoid

Do not call a commercial item a relic. Do not copy a mural fragment onto a sacred object without context. Do not claim that a particular color, crystal, or Buddha image guarantees protection. When in doubt, use a landscape, a map, or a written quotation about care rather than an image you cannot explain.
