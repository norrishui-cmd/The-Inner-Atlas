## Begin with the person, not the stone

A meaningful spiritual gift should respect the recipient's beliefs and style. Do not assign a diagnosis, destiny or spiritual need to someone else. Begin with a quality they have expressed or a transition they have chosen to share.

## Four gift intentions

- **Courage:** for a deliberate beginning or difficult transition
- **Clarity:** for study, reflection or a decision requiring attention
- **Renewal:** for rest, return and reconnecting with what sustains
- **Direction:** for a journey, new role or uncertain next chapter

## Bracelet, necklace or crystal?

A bracelet is visible to the wearer and works well as a repeated tactile reminder. A necklace can carry a more specific symbol close to the body. A crystal or stone object suits someone who prefers a private desk, pocket or meditation practice rather than visible jewelry.

## What to include with the gift

The most valuable card explains the cultural source, the intention you had in mind, and leaves the recipient free to interpret or simply enjoy the object. Avoid claiming it will heal, protect or change their fortune.
