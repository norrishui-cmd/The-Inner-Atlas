## The road as a practice of translation

The Silk Road was not one line on a map. It was a changing network of routes, oases, borders, languages, and negotiations. Dunhuang offers a useful modern metaphor only when we remember the people who made movement possible: travelers, translators, artists, patrons, caretakers, and local communities.

## A three-part practice

Before a trip or life transition, write down what you are carrying. During the crossing, ask what needs translation rather than defense. After arrival, record what changed in your understanding. This is a reflective practice inspired by history, not an ancient Silk Road ritual.
