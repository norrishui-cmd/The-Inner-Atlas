## From mural movement to a wearable cue

Feitian figures are celestial musicians and dancers in Dunhuang’s Buddhist visual world. Their flowing scarves, instruments, and suspended bodies make movement visible. A necklace inspired by them can therefore become a small cue to soften the shoulders, breathe, and make room for change.

## Context matters

Look for designs that credit Mogao imagery and avoid copying a single sacred figure as a talisman. The most respectful contemporary object makes its source legible, acknowledges interpretation, and does not claim to transmit divine power.

## A lightness practice

Touch the pendant at three transitions in your day. Let the gesture remind you to unclench the jaw, lengthen the exhale, and ask what can be carried more lightly. Record what changed rather than what you hope the object will do.
