## What is a Dunhuang Feitian?

Feitian is the Chinese term commonly used for the airborne celestial figures seen across Buddhist cave art, including the Mogao murals. Their flowing ribbons, musical instruments and movement are among the most recognizable visual languages associated with Dunhuang.

They should not be reduced to a generic “goddess of good luck.” Their imagery developed within Buddhist artistic and devotional contexts that changed across periods and caves.
