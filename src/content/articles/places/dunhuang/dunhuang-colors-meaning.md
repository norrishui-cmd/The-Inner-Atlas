## There is no single Dunhuang palette

The Mogao murals span roughly a millennium of Buddhist art. Period, workshop, subject, pigment, environment and later change all affect what we see. A modern palette labeled “Dunhuang colors” is therefore a selection and interpretation, not a timeless official color system.

## Why the colors feel distinctive

Earth reds, ochres, dark outlines, greens, blues and pale grounds often appear in reproductions, but digital images and present surfaces do not necessarily show the original appearance. Conservation context matters. Responsible design should avoid claiming that one color held the same spiritual meaning in every cave and century.

## Dunhuang Within material associations

For future collections we use clearly labeled contemporary associations:

- **Ochre and earth:** passage, endurance and the Desert Crossing
- **Deep blue and shadow:** silence, inward attention and the Hidden Cave
- **Green and water tones:** renewal and the Oasis Return
- **Layered mineral color:** exchange, movement and the Silk Road Path

These are brand design relationships, not reconstructed ancient doctrines.

## Choosing stones and metals by palette

Color may guide the choice of stone, glass, enamel, cord or metal finish, but visual resemblance does not prove historical material continuity or healing power. Every future product should state what it is actually made from.
