## What does feitian mean?

In modern Chinese discussion of Dunhuang art, *feitian*—literally “flying in the heavens”—refers to airborne celestial figures found across cave paintings. English descriptions may compare them with apsaras or other celestial beings, but terminology and iconography vary by historical context.

They often appear to move through space using streaming scarves, curved postures, music, flowers, and the rhythm of painted line. Their flight is visual rather than dependent on feathered wings.

## Why they feel so alive

The figures transform a rock-cut interior. Movement across walls and ceilings makes the bounded cave feel open and animated. Music and offering imagery connect beauty with a sacred setting.

## Contemporary reflection

For Dunhuang Within, feitian can become a modern prompt about lightness, release, and movement through change. This is our interpretation—not a claim that all historical flying figures carried one fixed psychological meaning.
