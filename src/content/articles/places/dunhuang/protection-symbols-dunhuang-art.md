## Protection is not one symbol

At the Mogao Caves, Buddhist figures, attendants, guardians, donors and patterned spaces are parts of complex religious images. Their roles cannot be reduced to a generic promise that an image will protect the wearer from every risk.

## From sacred setting to modern object

A responsible contemporary design begins by identifying the source and its setting. Then it states its own purpose plainly: perhaps a wearable reminder to move with care, keep a boundary, or return to courage. This is symbolic support, not insurance and not medical treatment.

## Choosing protection-inspired jewelry with care

Ask three questions: Is the source named? Is the religious language accurate? Does the seller avoid guarantees about destiny, safety or health? If those answers are unclear, the design may be using cultural imagery as decoration without responsibility.
