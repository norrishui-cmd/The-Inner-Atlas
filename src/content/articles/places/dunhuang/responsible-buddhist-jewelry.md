## Respect begins before purchase

Buddhist imagery appears across many regions, traditions and historical periods. A lotus, Buddha image, mantra and Dunhuang Feitian do not all mean the same thing. Responsible jewelry explains the specific relationship instead of flattening everything into “ancient Asian energy.”

## Seven questions to ask

1. What exactly is the symbol or text?
2. Which cultural or religious context does it come from?
3. Is the translation or transliteration checked?
4. Is the design copied from a sacred or restricted object?
5. Are materials and treatments disclosed?
6. Is a blessing claimed, and can that process be documented?
7. Are health, protection or fortune promises being presented as facts?

## Inspiration is not authenticity

“Inspired by” can be an honest description when a contemporary designer transforms a documented source. “Ancient,” “temple blessed” and “authentic” require evidence. A high price or spiritual vocabulary does not provide that evidence.
