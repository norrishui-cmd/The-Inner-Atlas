## What makes jewelry “Dunhuang inspired”?

It should begin with a traceable relationship to Dunhuang: the movement of a Feitian ribbon, a lotus seat in Buddhist imagery, the colors of mineral murals, the geometry of a cave ceiling, the contrast of desert and oasis, or the survival of a manuscript.

A generic Buddha charm becomes no more authentic by adding the name Dunhuang. Responsible design identifies the visual source, understands its religious and historical setting, and explains which part is a contemporary interpretation.

## From cultural symbol to personal intention

At Dunhuang Within, the bridge has four steps:

1. **Source:** a documented artwork, place or manuscript.
2. **Meaning:** what historians and the visual context can responsibly support.
3. **Reflection:** a clearly labeled modern theme such as courage, compassion or return.
4. **Practice:** a small repeated action supported by a bracelet, necklace or stone.

## Bracelets, necklaces and crystals

A bracelet can be a tactile pause because it meets the hand throughout the day. A pendant can keep one chosen symbol close to the body. A crystal can function as an object of attention through color, weight and touch. These are relationships and practices—not evidence that the material cures illness or controls events.
