## Pack for attention, not entertainment

The instinct on any trip is to bring enough to fill every idle minute. Quiet travel asks the opposite: pack light enough that boredom has room to become noticing.

## The short list

- **A paper notebook and pen.** Typing on a phone pulls you back into notifications; paper doesn't.
- **One physical book**, not a stack. Finishing it slowly is fine.
- **Comfortable, weather-appropriate layers** so discomfort doesn't cut a quiet sit short.
- **A physical map or printed itinerary**, so you are not defaulting to a phone screen to get oriented.
- **A watch**, if you have one, to check the time without opening your phone.

## What to leave behind or limit

Leave streaming downloads and podcasts off, or limit them to transit time only. Consider packing your phone charger separately from your bag's easiest pocket, so reaching for it takes a deliberate extra step.

## Practical, not ascetic

None of this requires giving up your phone entirely or traveling with nothing. The goal is friction: small choices that make distraction slightly less automatic, so attention becomes the easier default.
