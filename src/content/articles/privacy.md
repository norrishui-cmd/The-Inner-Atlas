*Last updated: July 16, 2026*

Dunhuang Within ("**Dunhuang Within**," "**we**," "**us**," or "**our**") publishes dunhuangwithin.com (the "**Site**"). This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, why we collect it, how third parties such as Google may use cookies to serve ads on the Site, and the rights available to you — including rights under the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation ("**GDPR**") and the California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act ("**CCPA/CPRA**").

By using the Site, you acknowledge the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Site.

## 1. Information we collect

**Information you provide directly.** If you submit an email address through our newsletter form, we collect that email address and the source page you submitted it from. We do not currently operate user accounts, and we do not ask for names, payment details, or other identifying information through the Site.

**Information collected automatically.** Like most websites, our server and analytics tools automatically log technical data when you visit, which may include your IP address, browser type and version, device type, operating system, referring/exit pages, pages viewed, and timestamps.

**Cookies and similar technologies.** We and our third-party partners use cookies, pixels, and local storage to operate the Site, understand aggregate usage, and (where enabled) serve advertising. See Section 3 below.

**The Personal Retreat Builder.** This interactive tool runs entirely in your browser. Your answers are stored locally on your device and are **not** transmitted to our servers by default. If analytics is enabled, we may record that a step was completed (e.g., an anonymous event name), but not the text of your responses.

## 2. How we use information

We use the information described above to: operate, maintain, and improve the Site; understand which content and paths are useful, in aggregate; send newsletter emails to subscribers who opted in, and let them unsubscribe at any time; detect, prevent, and address technical issues, fraud, or abuse; and, where advertising is enabled, allow advertising partners to serve and measure ads as described in Section 3.

We do not sell personal information for money, and we do not use your data to make automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects on you.

## 3. Cookies, analytics & advertising (including Google AdSense)

**Analytics.** We use analytics tooling (such as Google Analytics/Google Tag Manager style event tracking) to understand aggregate traffic and engagement. This data is generally reported in aggregate and is not used to identify you personally.

**Advertising cookies.** This Site may display advertising served by Google and other third-party advertising vendors ("**Vendors**"). Vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to this Site and other sites across the internet. Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads based on your visits to this Site and/or other sites.

- You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting [Google Ads Settings](https://adssettings.google.com/).
- You may opt out of some third-party vendors' use of cookies for personalized advertising by visiting [www.aboutads.info/choices](https://www.aboutads.info/choices/).
- More detail on how Google uses data from sites that use its services is available at [Google's "How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services"](https://policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites) and [Google's advertising technologies page](https://policies.google.com/technologies/ads).

**Cookie choices.** Where required by law (see Section 4), we will only set non-essential advertising and analytics cookies after you provide consent through an on-site cookie banner or consent tool. You can also control cookies through your browser settings, including blocking or deleting them, though this may affect Site functionality.

## 4. Your rights under the GDPR (EEA, UK & Switzerland)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have rights under the GDPR / UK GDPR, including the right to:

- **Access** the personal data we hold about you;
- **Rectify** inaccurate or incomplete data;
- **Erase** your personal data ("right to be forgotten"), subject to certain exceptions;
- **Restrict** or **object** to certain processing, including processing based on legitimate interests and processing for direct marketing;
- **Data portability**, to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format;
- **Withdraw consent** at any time where processing is based on consent (e.g., non-essential cookies), without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal; and
- **Lodge a complaint** with your local data protection supervisory authority.

**Legal bases for processing.** We rely on: (a) **consent**, for non-essential cookies and advertising personalization; (b) **legitimate interests**, for core analytics and Site security, balanced against your rights; (c) **contract**, to deliver a newsletter you asked to receive; and (d) **legal obligation**, where applicable.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details in Section 9. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request.

## 5. Your rights under the CCPA/CPRA (California residents)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

- **Know** what categories of personal information we collect, use, and disclose, and the purposes for doing so (see Sections 1–3 above);
- **Delete** personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions;
- **Correct** inaccurate personal information;
- **Opt out of the "sale" or "sharing"** of personal information. We do not sell personal information for monetary consideration. However, the use of advertising cookies described in Section 3 may be considered "sharing" for cross-context behavioral advertising under the CPRA. You can opt out via the links in Section 3 or by contacting us using the details in Section 9;
- **Limit the use of sensitive personal information**, though we do not knowingly collect sensitive personal information as defined by the CCPA/CPRA; and
- **Non-discrimination** for exercising any of these rights.

You (or an authorized agent) may submit a request using the contact details in Section 9. We will verify the request based on the information available to us before responding.

## 6. Children's privacy

The Site is not directed to children under 13 (or under 16 where a higher age applies under local law), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will take steps to delete it.

## 7. Data retention & security

We retain personal information (such as newsletter email addresses) only as long as needed for the purposes described in this policy, or as required by law, and delete or anonymize it thereafter. We use reasonable administrative and technical safeguards to protect data, but no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

## 8. International data transfers & third-party links

Our Site and service providers (including analytics and advertising Vendors) may process data in countries other than your own, including the United States. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) for such transfers.

The Site may link to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of sites we do not operate; please review their own privacy policies.

## 9. Contact us & changes to this policy

Questions about this Privacy Policy, or requests to exercise the rights described above, can be sent to **privacy@dunhuangwithin.com**.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above. Continued use of the Site after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
