## Decide the length before you start

An open-ended break is easy to abandon. Choose a specific length—a weekend, a week, a month—and write it down somewhere you'll see it.

## Make it structurally harder to break

Log out rather than staying logged in "just in case." Remove the apps from your phone rather than only muting notifications. If your work requires a platform, use a browser only, at set times, rather than the app.

## Tell people, briefly

A short note to close contacts or an away message on the platform itself prevents the break from creating confusion, and reduces the temptation to check "just to reply to one thing."

## Plan for the dips

The second and third day are often harder than the first, as the habit loop still expects the reward. Having an alternative activity ready for the exact times you'd normally scroll—commutes, waiting rooms, before bed—matters more than willpower alone.

## Returning without relapsing

Before logging back in, decide which accounts or habits you actually want to keep, and consider unfollowing or muting anything that mainly triggered comparison or reactivity. A break is most useful when the return looks different from what came before.
