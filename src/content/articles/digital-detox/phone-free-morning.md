## Start the night before

A phone-free morning is decided the night before, not in the moment. Charge your phone outside the bedroom, and use a separate alarm clock or clock radio if it currently doubles as your alarm.

## The first hour

Decide in advance what replaces the phone: water, light stretching, a short walk, or simply sitting with a coffee and no screen. Having a specific alternative reduces the pull to check "just quickly."

## When the urge hits

The urge to check will happen, especially in the first few days. Naming it silently—"that's the habit, not a real need"—and returning to your chosen activity works better than trying to suppress the urge entirely.

## What to do about anxiety over missing something

If you're worried about missing an urgent message, agree with close contacts on a separate channel (a phone call, not a notification) for genuine emergencies, and check your phone once, at a set time, rather than leaving it open all morning.

## Building it into a longer practice

A phone-free morning pairs well with a broader [digital detox](/digital-detox/) or a [personal retreat morning routine](/personal-retreat/morning-routine/) if you want to extend the practice further.
