## Why vague goals fail

"Use my phone less" has no clear success condition, so it's easy to break without noticing. Specific, situational rules are easier to keep because you know immediately whether you followed them.

## Rules worth trying

- **Phone-free rooms.** Choose one room—often the bedroom or dining table—where the phone never goes, no exceptions.
- **Set check-in times.** Check messages three times a day at fixed times rather than whenever a notification arrives.
- **No phone for the first and last hour** of the day.
- **One tap, one purpose.** Open the phone with a specific task in mind; if you notice yourself drifting to another app, close it and return to the original task.
- **A physical off switch.** Turn on airplane mode, rather than relying on willpower, during protected blocks of time.

## Choose two, not all five

Trying every rule at once usually collapses within a few days. Pick one or two that address your actual pattern—mornings, meals, or bedtime—and hold those consistently before adding more.

## When a rule needs adjusting

If a rule keeps getting broken, it may be poorly matched to your situation rather than a sign of weak willpower. Adjust the rule itself before abandoning the practice—see our broader [digital detox guide](/digital-detox/) for more structured options.
